## 77. Primitive Transfer Element Gate

This section defines what would count as a derived transition transfer element:

$$
\mathcal T_{ji}^{(\eta)}.
$$

It also records a negative result:

$$
\Gamma_\eta=\Gamma\circ K_\eta
$$

does not by itself define an oriented transition \(i\to j\).

`primitive_transfer_element_gate_initialized`

### 77.1. Why positivity is insufficient

The inherited kernel update:

$$
\Gamma_\eta
=
\Gamma\circ K_\eta
$$

changes distinguishability and coherence between histories.

It does not specify which readout cell at the source is transported into which readout cell at the target.

Therefore:

$$
\Gamma_I,K_\eta
\nRightarrow
\mathcal T_{ji}^{(\eta)}
$$

unless the inheritance act also generates transition support.

This is the same obstruction seen earlier:

positive kernels constrain magnitudes, but do not select phases or oriented transfer.

### 77.2. Minimal data for a derived transfer

Let:

$$
A_i,A_j\in\mathcal E
$$

be admissible source/target readout cells.

A derived transfer needs an event-transport map or relation:

$$
\Pi_\eta:A_i\leadsto \Pi_\eta A_i,
$$

generated by the inheritance act \(\eta\).

Then a normalized coherence transfer can be defined as:

$$
\mathcal T_{ji}^{(\eta)}
=
\frac{
\mathcal A_\eta(A_j,\Pi_\eta A_i)
}{
\sqrt{
\mu_\eta(A_j)
\mu_\eta(\Pi_\eta A_i)
}
}.
$$

This same-slice expression is a proxy.

It is a full oriented transfer only when the update supplies a canonical identification between the transported source and the after-update event layer.

The complete before-after version requires a cross-update kernel \(\Xi_\eta\).

This is defined only when:

$$
\mu_\eta(A_j)>0,
\qquad
\mu_\eta(\Pi_\eta A_i)>0.
$$

By positivity of \(\mathcal A_\eta\):

$$
|\mathcal T_{ji}^{(\eta)}|
\le
1.
$$

### 77.3. Transfer phase

If:

$$
|\mathcal T_{ji}^{(\eta)}|>0,
$$

the transition phase readout is:

$$
U_{ji}^{(\eta)}
=
\frac{\mathcal T_{ji}^{(\eta)}}{|\mathcal T_{ji}^{(\eta)}|}.
$$

The edge phase is:

$$
\vartheta_{ji}^{(\eta)}
=
\operatorname{Arg}U_{ji}^{(\eta)}.
$$

The phase is undefined when the transfer magnitude vanishes.

Zero-transfer edges cannot be used as phase carriers.

`transfer_phase_defined_only_for_nonzero_transfer`

### 77.4. Gauge covariance

Endpoint readout relabeling:

$$
A_i\mapsto e^{i\alpha_i}A_i,
\qquad
A_j\mapsto e^{i\alpha_j}A_j
$$

must transform:

$$
\mathcal T_{ji}^{(\eta)}
\mapsto
e^{i\alpha_j}
\mathcal T_{ji}^{(\eta)}
e^{-i\alpha_i}.
$$

Therefore:

$$
U_{ji}^{(\eta)}
\mapsto
e^{i\alpha_j}
U_{ji}^{(\eta)}
e^{-i\alpha_i}.
$$

Only closed products are gauge invariant:

$$
U_\gamma
=
\prod_{(i\to j)\in\gamma}
U_{ji}^{(\eta)}.
$$

### 77.5. Composition gate

For composable inheritance acts:

$$
\eta_2\circ\eta_1,
$$

transfer readout must satisfy an admissible-context composition law:

$$
\mathcal T_{ki}^{(\eta_2\circ\eta_1)}
\approx
\sum_j
\mathcal T_{kj}^{(\eta_2)}
\mathcal T_{ji}^{(\eta_1)}.
$$

The approximation sign is deliberate.

It allows loss into unobserved cells and failure of the chosen intermediate partition to be complete.

Exact composition is required only when the intermediate readout cells form a complete probability-admissible transition context.

### 77.6. Reciprocity gate

If the theory declares a reverse update:

$$
\bar\eta,
$$

then reversal must satisfy:

$$
\mathcal T_{\bar i\bar j}^{(\bar\eta)}
=
\overline{\mathcal T_{ji}^{(\eta)}}
$$

for the corresponding reversed cells.

Consequently:

$$
U_{\bar i\bar j}^{(\bar\eta)}
=
\overline{U_{ji}^{(\eta)}}.
$$

This gives:

$$
\vartheta_{\bar i\bar j}^{(\bar\eta)}
=
-\vartheta_{ji}^{(\eta)}
\mod2\pi.
$$

`transfer_reciprocity_gate_defined`

### 77.7. Derived / parametric / fitted classification

The transfer element is `derived_transfer` only if all of the following are fixed before experimental comparison:

1. admissible cells \(A_i,A_j\);
2. event transport \(\Pi_\eta\);
3. updated weights \(W_\eta\);
4. updated kernel \(\Gamma_\eta\);
5. normalization convention for null events.

It is `parametric_transfer` if one or more of these objects is left free but not tuned to data.

It is `fitted_transfer` if phases, branches, cells, or transports are selected after comparison with \(G_N\), \(\hbar\), \(\omega_{\ell,G}\), spectroscopy, or interference data.

Only `derived_transfer` can close the primitive phase route.

### 77.8. Known gates

In a smooth quantum readout limit, the transfer phase must reduce to the standard propagator phase gate:

$$
K(x_f,t_f;x_i,t_i)
\sim
\int_{\gamma:i\to f}
e^{iS[\gamma]/\hbar}
\mathcal D\gamma.
$$

For a short-time Hamiltonian readout:

$$
\mathcal T_{ji}
\approx
\delta_{ji}
-
\frac{i}{\hbar}H_{ji}\Delta t.
$$

For a two-path context:

$$
\Delta\phi
=
\operatorname{Arg}
\left(
\mathcal T_1\overline{\mathcal T_2}
\right)
$$

must be the phase entering:

$$
P\propto1+V\cos\Delta\phi.
$$

These are gates for checking the transfer construction.

They are not allowed as sources for choosing \(\mathcal T_{ji}^{(\eta)}\).

### 77.9. No-fit rule

Forbidden:

1. choose \(\Pi_\eta\) to force a desired cycle phase;
2. choose \(\mathcal T_{ji}\) as a free complex matrix and call it primitive;
3. drop cells whose phases spoil the fixed point after comparison;
4. use \(G_N\), Planck units, or observed \(\hbar\) to set transition phases.

Allowed:

1. define \(\Pi_\eta\) from the inheritance act before comparison;
2. classify the route as `parametric_transfer` when \(\Pi_\eta\) is not derived;
3. reject the fixed-point route if all admissible derived transfers are exact or zero-phase;
4. use known phase experiments only as post-derivation gates.

### 77.10. What is closed

Closed:

1. positive kernel update alone does not define \(\mathcal T_{ji}\);
2. a derived transfer requires event transport \(\Pi_\eta\);
3. normalized transfer is bounded by positivity;
4. transfer phase is meaningful only for nonzero transfer;
5. composition, reciprocity, and gauge covariance are required gates.

Open:

1. primitive derivation of \(\Pi_\eta\);
2. explicit \(W_\eta,\Gamma_\eta\) for clock-vacuum transitions;
3. derived non-exact cycle holonomies;
4. independent action-cost readout \(C_{ij}\);
5. numerical \(\hbar_I\), \(\omega_{\ell,I}\), and \(G_I\).
