## 76. Primitive Transition Phase Readout

This section narrows the missing object behind the fixed-point route.

The missing object is not a new gravity parameter.

It is the primitive transition phase:

$$
U_{ij}
=
U_I(\eta_j\leftarrow\eta_i)
\in U(1).
$$

### 76.1. Edge phase before dimensional action

For an allowed grammar transition:

$$
(a_i,a_j)\in\mathcal P_{EF},
$$

the first target is the dimensionless edge phase:

$$
U_{ij}
=
e^{i\vartheta_{ij}}.
$$

The dimensional action increment is secondary:

$$
\mathfrak s_{ij}
=
\hbar_I\vartheta_{ij}
\mod2\pi\hbar_I.
$$

Therefore the fixed-point phase route can be split into two problems:

1. derive \(U_{ij}\);
2. derive the conversion scale \(\hbar_I\).

This prevents a circular derivation where \(\hbar_I\) is used to define the phase that is later used to derive \(\hbar_I\).

### 76.2. Normalized transition readout

Let \(\mathcal T_{ij}\) be a primitive complex transition element generated by the update rule.

When:

$$
|\mathcal T_{ij}|>0,
$$

define:

$$
U_{ij}
=
\frac{\mathcal T_{ij}}{|\mathcal T_{ij}|}.
$$

This removes coherence magnitude and keeps only phase readout.

If \(\mathcal T_{ij}\) is inserted as a free complex edge label, the route is not closed.

Closure requires:

$$
\mathcal I,\Gamma_I,K_\eta
\Rightarrow
\mathcal T_{ij}
\Rightarrow
U_{ij}.
$$

### 76.3. Gauge covariance

Local endpoint phase relabeling:

$$
a_i\mapsto e^{i\alpha_i}a_i
$$

changes edge phase readout as:

$$
U_{ij}
\mapsto
e^{i\alpha_j}U_{ij}e^{-i\alpha_i}.
$$

Equivalently:

$$
\vartheta_{ij}
\mapsto
\vartheta_{ij}+\alpha_j-\alpha_i.
$$

Therefore an individual edge phase is gauge-dependent.

For a closed cycle:

$$
\gamma=(i_0i_1\cdots i_Li_0),
$$

the cycle holonomy is:

$$
U_\gamma
=
\prod_{r=0}^{L}
U_{i_ri_{r+1}},
$$

with \(i_{L+1}=i_0\).

Under endpoint relabeling:

$$
U_\gamma
\mapsto
U_\gamma.
$$

Only:

$$
\operatorname{Arg}U_\gamma
$$

is a physical phase input for the fixed-point map.

### 76.4. Non-exact phase condition

If:

$$
U_{ij}
=
e^{i(\alpha_j-\alpha_i)},
$$

then:

$$
U_\gamma=1
$$

for every closed cycle.

This is the exact cocycle case.

It gives no nontrivial rotation source.

A nonzero fixed-point rotation requires at least one closed cycle with:

$$
\operatorname{Arg}U_\gamma
\ne
0
\mod2\pi.
$$

### 76.5. Relation to \(\hbar_I\)

The phase readout alone gives:

$$
\vartheta_{ij}
=
\operatorname{Arg}U_{ij}.
$$

It does not give the dimensional value:

$$
\hbar_I.
$$

To extract \(\hbar_I\) without fitting, the theory needs an independent primitive action-cost readout:

$$
C_{ij}
$$

that is not defined through \(\hbar_I\).

Then the required consistency condition is:

$$
\frac{C_{ij}}{\vartheta_{ij}}
=
\hbar_I
$$

for all admissible nonzero transition phases, modulo the correct \(2\pi\) branch.

For cycles this is cleaner:

$$
\frac{C_\gamma}{\operatorname{Arg}U_\gamma}
=
\hbar_I.
$$

The no-fit residual is:

$$
\mathcal R_{\hbar,I}(\gamma,\gamma')
=
\left|
\frac{
C_\gamma/\operatorname{Arg}U_\gamma
}{
C_{\gamma'}/\operatorname{Arg}U_{\gamma'}
}
-1
\right|.
$$

Closure target:

$$
\mathcal R_{\hbar,I}\to0
$$

across independently derived admissible cycles.

$$

The readout probability must reduce to:

$$
P

For charged holonomy:

$$
\Delta\phi_{AB}

must be recovered.

For smooth wave readouts:

$$
p=\hbar k,
\qquad
E=\hbar\omega
$$

must remain the continuum spectral gates.

These gates test the phase-action bridge.

They are not allowed as post-hoc sources for choosing \(U_{ij}\) in the gravity route.

4. define \(C_{ij}\) using \(\hbar_I\) and then claim \(\hbar_I=C_{ij}/\vartheta_{ij}\).

Allowed:

1. derive \(U_{ij}\) from the primitive update transfer;
2. reject exact cocycles as non-rotating;
3. carry \(\hbar_I\) as open until independent action cost exists;
4. test phase gates only after the derivation is fixed.
