### 7.4. Validated-domain rule

GR, quantum mechanics, and \(\Lambda\)CDM are not axioms of the protolanguage.

They are validated-domain gates.

The theory must reproduce their confirmed formulas where those formulas are experimentally secure.

It must not assume that the same readout remains exact in every regime.

Domain rule:

$$
\mathcal T_{\mathrm{proto}}
\to
\mathcal T_{\mathrm{known}}
$$

only on the tested domain:

$$
\mathcal D_{\mathrm{tested}}
$$

Outside that domain, deviations are allowed only as explicit residual fields:

$$
\mathcal R_I
\neq
0
$$

and every residual must be tied to an experimental gate.

Accepted weak-field gates:

$$
\frac{\Delta f}{f}
\approx
\frac{\Delta\Phi}{c^2}
$$

$$
\Delta\Phi
=
4\pi G\rho
$$

$$
\Delta\theta
=
\frac{4GM}{c^2b}
$$

$$

=
\frac{2|a_A-a_B|}{|a_A+a_B|}
\le
\epsilon_{\mathrm{WEP}}
$$

\frac{\Lambda c^2}{3}

This formula is a fit target in its validated domain, not a primitive truth.
