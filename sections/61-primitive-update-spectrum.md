## 61. Primitive Update Spectrum

This section defines the primitive update spectrum route to:

$$
\omega_{\ell,I}.
$$

It does not compute the spectrum numerically.

It specifies the operator whose spectrum must be computed.

### 61.1. Local update sector

For a clock-vacuum link \(\langle EF\rangle\), let:

$$
\mathcal U_{EF}
\subset
\mathcal I
$$

be the admissible local inherited updates that affect the clock-strain kernel.

Each update has a positive mode representation:

$$
K_{\eta,EF}
\succeq0.
$$

The local update sector is:

$$
\mathscr H_{EF}
=
\overline{\mathrm{span}}
\{
|\eta\rangle:\eta\in\mathcal U_{EF}
\}.
$$

This is a calculational Hilbert/Gram readout space generated by the positive kernel sector, not a primitive quantum Hilbert space.

### 61.2. Update transfer operator

Define the flat-vacuum one-step update transfer operator:

$$
\mathsf T_{EF}
:
\mathscr H_{EF}
\to
\mathscr H_{EF}.
$$

Matrix elements are inherited from transition weights between admissible updates:

$$
\langle \eta'|\mathsf T_{EF}|\eta\rangle
=
\mathcal T_{EF}(\eta'\leftarrow\eta).
$$

Admissibility gates:

1. positivity of the induced kernel update;
2. stationarity in the flat clock-vacuum sector;
3. reciprocity under \(E\leftrightarrow F\);
4. clock-readout normalization independent of \(G_N\).

### 61.3. Spectral phases and clock time

Let:

$$
\lambda_n
$$

be eigenvalues of \(\mathsf T_{EF}\).

For stable oscillatory modes:

$$
\lambda_n
=
e^{-i\theta_n-\gamma_n},
\qquad
\gamma_n\ge0.
$$

Let:

$$
\Delta\tau_{\mathrm{step}}
$$

be the flat clock-readout duration of one transfer step.

Then the mode frequency is:

$$
\omega_n
=
\frac{|\theta_n|}
{\Delta\tau_{\mathrm{step}}}.
$$

### 61.4. Link frequency from first stable mode

Define:

$$
\omega_{\ell,I}
=
\min_{n\neq0}
\left\{
\omega_n:
\gamma_n\le\epsilon_\gamma,
\;
n\text{ couples to }s_{EF}
\right\}.
$$

The zero mode \(n=0\) is the flat vacuum identity/update-equilibrium mode and is excluded.

If no stable mode couples to \(s_{EF}\), the clock-vacuum pole route fails.

`omega_link_from_update_spectrum`

### 61.5. Spectrum-response equivalence

The response function may be written spectrally:

$$
\mathcal R_\chi(\omega)
=
\sum_{n\neq0}
\frac{g_n^2}
{\omega_n^2-\omega^2-i\Gamma_n\omega}.
$$

where:

$$
g_n
$$

is the coupling of mode \(n\) to clock strain \(s_{EF}\).

Then the first response pole agrees with the update-spectrum definition if:

$$
\omega_{\ell,I}
=
\min_{n:g_n\neq0}
\omega_n.
$$

### 61.6. No-fit rule

Forbidden:

1. choose \(\Delta\tau_{\mathrm{step}}\) from \(G_N\);
2. choose \(\theta_n\) to hit \(\omega_{\ell,G}\);
3. discard a lower stable strain-coupled mode because it spoils \(G_I\);
4. change \(\mathcal U_{EF}\) after comparing with experiments.

Allowed:

1. derive \(\mathsf T_{EF}\) from the inherited update rules;
2. compute its spectrum;
3. identify the first stable strain-coupled mode;
4. compare the resulting \(\omega_{\ell,I}\) with the gravity-gate target.

### 61.7. What is closed

Closed as a formal route:

$$
\mathsf T_{EF}
\Rightarrow
\{\lambda_n\}
\Rightarrow
\omega_{\ell,I}.
$$

Open:

1. explicit transition weights \(\mathcal T_{EF}\);
2. flat step duration \(\Delta\tau_{\mathrm{step}}\);
3. coupling coefficients \(g_n\);
4. numerical first mode;
5. proof that the first mode is universal across clock species.
